The feeling

Christmas deserves a home worthy of it

For a certain kind of person, Christmas isn't just a holiday. It's a project, a passion, a creative act that begins in September and reaches its crescendo on Christmas morning. She has opinions about garland placement. She photographs her table settings. She spends weeks planning the perfect gifts for twenty people she loves. She is looking, every October, for a place that gets it — that understands the depth of her enthusiasm and meets it with something beautiful.

Right now that place doesn't exist. She cobbles together Pinterest boards, Facebook groups, scattered apps, and generic gift guides. She deserves better. Figgy Pudding is better.

The feeling we're building toward: the moment you open a beautiful magazine and feel immediately at home in its world. The warmth of a trusted friend who shares your taste. The satisfaction of a perfectly organised project that also happens to be joyful. That is what Figgy Pudding should feel like every time she opens it.

The aesthetic

An editorial standard, not a single style

Figgy Pudding is not a Victorian Christmas site. It is not a cottagecore site. It is not a farmhouse site. It is a site built to the standard of a premium lifestyle publication — the kind of editorial judgment and visual quality you see in Architectural Digest, Homeworthy, or Better Homes and Gardens — applied to Christmas in all its forms.

The agent

The Christmas companion that knows you

Imagine opening Figgy Pudding in October and being greeted by something that already knows your gift list has 23 people on it, that you're hosting Christmas dinner for 14 this year, that your aesthetic is traditional with a Scandinavian influence, and that you set a reminder last November to make the wreath tutorial you saved in 2025. It knows your budget, your traditions, your tastes, and your timeline. It is ready to help you do Christmas rather than just find information about it.

That is what the Figgy Pudding agent will be. Not a search engine. Not a generic chatbot. A genuine Christmas planning companion — warm, knowledgeable, deeply personal, and built on a knowledge base that no general-purpose AI has access to.

Generic AI search

The Figgy Pudding agent
Knows Christmas deeply and specifically

Filtered through your aesthetic preferences
Connects what you see to what you can do

The technical foundation is a RAG architecture — Retrieval Augmented Generation — in which the agent draws from Figgy Pudding's own curated database of Christmas vendors, events, traditions, and member knowledge rather than from the open internet. When a member asks "find me a Christmas tree farm within 30 miles that specialises in Fraser firs and has a hot chocolate bar," the agent searches Figgy Pudding's verified local vendor database, not Google. That database — built and maintained by the platform, enriched by the community, curated to a standard — is a genuine competitive moat that no general-purpose AI can replicate.

Key Insights

What the model reveals about the business.

Membership is the anchor, not the ceiling
Membership fees represent 77% of Year 1 revenue but fall to just 15% in Year 5 (high scenario) as affiliate commerce, print on demand, the storefront, and advertising scale with traffic. The business becomes progressively less dependent on membership as it matures.
Traffic is the real multiplier
Six of ten revenue streams scale with site traffic, not member count. Affiliate links, print on demand, the storefront, and display advertising are all open to non-members. At 1.75M monthly sessions in Year 5, the non-member audience generates more revenue than the member base alone.
Phase 2–3 retail streams are where serious money lives
The curated storefront ($1.4M), affiliate commerce ($2.2M), and print on demand ($1.4M) collectively dwarf membership in the high scenario by Year 5. These streams require audience scale to unlock but compound rapidly once reached — directly analogous to The Knot's retail model.
Brand partnerships are high-margin and underestimated
$392k in Year 5 (high scenario) from 14 partnerships at $28k average value. Near-100% margin once audience is established. This stream grows with reputation and trust, not just traffic — making it more durable than ad-supported revenue.
The modest scenario is the honest base case
The modest scenario — 12,000 members and $1.21M annual revenue by Year 5 — assumes steady but unspectacular execution: good content, growing Pinterest presence, a community gaining momentum. It does not require viral moments or press coverage. It requires consistency over four years. This is the number to plan against.

Capital
Minimal. Buildable with no-code tools (Squarespace/Showit + Memberful) and AI-assisted content production. Domain secured. Phase 1 requires primarily time and creative output.
Content
54 editorial articles at launch across three pillars. 3–4 articles per week from July through September 2026. AI-assisted research and drafting with founder editorial judgment and voice.
Audience
Pinterest (primary organic channel), Facebook community group, email list with lead magnet. All buildable before site launch. Affiliate infrastructure (Amazon Associates, ShareASale) live before first article published.
Timeline
Phase 1: founding members and live site by Christmas 2026. Phase 2 (full retail, community): 2027. Phase 3 (local discovery, brand partnerships, UK): 2027–2028.
