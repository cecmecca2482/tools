Part One

Getting More Eyes on Your Work

Right now, most people who find you are either already following you or stumble across you by luck. This section is about changing that — systematically and passively, so your work finds the people who will love it most.

🔍 Etsy Search Optimization

When someone searches Etsy for "whimsical dragon art print" or "hand painted wooden box," Etsy decides which listings to show based on titles, descriptions, and tags. Right now your listings may not be fully optimized for how buyers actually search.

What we'll do — you won't touch a thing:

  • Rewrite every listing title to lead with searchable terms buyers actually use
  • Rewrite descriptions to weave in words people search for naturally
  • Fill in all 13 tags on every listing — theme, style, occasion, aesthetic, recipient
Example: "THE BOOKDRAGON - Print of Original Acrylic Painting by Carolee Clark" becomes "Book Dragon Art Print — Fantasy Dragon in Library — Gift for Book Lovers — Whimsical Dragon Wall Art — 11x14"

📌 Pinterest

Pinterest is essentially a visual search engine — and one of the best platforms in the world for handmade art, whimsical aesthetics, fantasy themes, and cottagecore imagery. Your work is perfectly suited for it.

  • Set up a King of Mice Studios Pinterest presence from scratch
  • Create themed boards — Halloween, seasonal, fantasy, animals, witchy, holiday
  • Pin your existing work with optimized descriptions linked to your Etsy shop
  • Let it run permanently in the background — every pin is a lasting advertisement
Pinterest drives passive Etsy traffic indefinitely. Set it up once, and it works while you sleep.

📸 Instagram — More Consistently

You're already on Instagram but posting less than on Facebook. Since Meta owns both, posting to both simultaneously takes one click — no extra effort at all on your part.

  • Connect Facebook and Instagram so posts share automatically
  • Manage posting timing to maximize reach on both platforms
  • Optimize captions and hashtags for Instagram's discovery algorithm

🎬 Process Videos — Reels & TikTok

You already do occasional Reels of yourself painting — and that's the single most powerful content you can create. Videos of an artist creating something magical consistently stop people mid-scroll and attract brand new audiences.

  • Film you painting using just a phone propped up nearby — you just paint normally
  • Edit footage into short, beautiful time-lapses with music
  • Post consistently across Facebook Reels, Instagram Reels, and TikTok
  • Manage the TikTok account entirely — you won't need to be on it at all
You never need to show your face. Hands, paintbrush, the piece coming to life — that's all anyone needs to see. And it is mesmerizing to watch.

Part Two

Growing Your Collector Base

Your current collectors are loyal and wonderful — but they've been collecting for decades and many already have dozens of your pieces. Building the next generation of collectors is essential for long-term growth.

🌿 Reaching Younger Audiences

There are enormous online communities that already love exactly what you make — they just don't know you exist yet:

  • Book lovers and fantasy readers — the BookTok and BookDragon communities
  • Pagan, witchy, and spiritual aesthetics — massive on Etsy, Instagram, and TikTok
  • Cottagecore and fairycore — one of the fastest growing aesthetics online right now
  • Halloween obsessives — a devoted, year-round community, not just seasonal
  • Tolkien and fantasy collectors — passionate and willing to pay for quality
  • Folk art and decorative painting collectors
Your 30 years of devoted collectors is powerful social proof to a younger buyer. It signals you're the real thing — not a trend, not someone who started six months ago.

✉️ Building an Email List

Right now your reach depends entirely on Facebook deciding to show your posts. That's fragile — as you've experienced when Facebook limits your reach. An email list is something you own outright. No algorithm can touch it.

  • Set up a simple, free email list using a tool like Mailchimp
  • Invite your most devoted followers to join for exclusive "first look" access
  • Send a simple email each time you have new work — just the image and a link
  • Managed entirely by Courtney — you never have to touch it
First look access is genuinely valuable to collectors who've missed pieces before. It creates deep loyalty and costs nothing to offer.

Part Three

New Revenue Streams

Additional ways to earn income from work you're already creating — most requiring little to no ongoing effort once set up.

⭐ Most Exciting

🌙 Patreon — Exclusive Collector Membership

This requires almost nothing extra from you — and could become your most reliable monthly income.

One or two pieces per month are designated as Patreon-only — never offered to the general public. Devoted collectors pay a monthly membership fee for exclusive access. They're not just supporting an artist — they're part of an inner circle with access to work nobody else can buy.

  • Supporter tier ($5–10/month) — behind the scenes glimpses, works in progress, the story behind each piece
  • Collector tier ($25–35/month) — access to Patreon-exclusive pieces, first right of purchase, occasional works-in-progress glimpses

You don't create anything extra. You simply set one or two pieces aside as Patreon-only each month. That's a decision, not extra work.

$900+

Just 30 collector tier subscribers at $30/month — on top of everything else — for work you were going to create anyway.

Courtney sets up and manages the Patreon entirely. You just paint.

Your images can live on throw pillows, tote bags, mugs, phone cases, and puzzles through platforms like Society6.

Adding 5x7 and 8x10 options opens completely new buyers — people who love your work but need a smaller price point.

💾 Digital Downloads

Printable art files that buyers print themselves. Upload once, sell forever. Nearly 100% margin after Etsy's small fee.

  • Zero fulfillment cost or effort
  • Sells while you sleep
  • Courtney handles all file prep and listing creation

🐾 Commissions — A Formalized Policy

You already take commissions on your own terms — and that doesn't need to change. But there's an opportunity to make them more structured, more predictable, and more financially meaningful.

The idea: at the start of each year, announce a finite number of commission slots — for example, 10 commissions available for the year, at specific sizes and set prices. Once they're gone, they're gone until next year.

  • You know exactly how many commissions you're agreeing to — no more, no less
  • Buyers know the terms upfront — size, price, your creative control
  • Scarcity makes the slots genuinely desirable — devoted collectors will claim them quickly
  • You can calculate guaranteed commission income before the year even begins
If 10 commissions at an average of $300 each means $3,000 in guaranteed income on January 1st — that's a meaningful number. Courtney can help write and post the annual commission announcement. You just approve it.

🃏 Greeting Cards — Modernized

Right now you assemble card sets by hand — printing, taping, packaging, hand-writing labels, post office trips. That's an enormous amount of physical labor. There's a much better way.

  • Print on demand services like Printful produce finished, folded greeting cards and ship directly to your customers
  • Customer orders on Etsy → order goes automatically to fulfillment → they handle everything → you never touch it
  • Slightly lower margin, but hours of exhausting physical assembly simply disappear
Trading a small margin for the elimination of exhausting physical labor is absolutely worth it.

Part Four

Working Smarter

This section has nothing to do with making more money. It's about eliminating the tedious, physically demanding tasks that eat into your time and energy every single week.

⭐ High Impact

📦 Shipping Labels — No More Hand-Writing

Right now you hand-write every shipping label and make multiple trips to the post office every week. This is completely unnecessary.

The solution is a free tool called Pirateship:

  • Type or paste the customer's address
  • Select the package size and weight
  • Print a professional shipping label instantly
  • Schedule a free USPS pickup from your front door — no post office trips at all

The mail carrier comes to you. You hand over the packages. That's it.

Pirateship also offers discounted USPS rates — better than counter rates — so you'd save money on every shipment too. Requires a basic printer (~$80–120, one-time). Courtney will set everything up so the first time you use it, it just works.

⚙️ Print Order Fulfillment — Automated

Every time a print sells on Etsy, you manually type the customer's name, address, and order details into your fulfillment company's system. That's completely automatable.

  • Connect your Etsy shop to your fulfillment company using automation tools
  • Every order gets submitted automatically — no manual entry
  • You receive a confirmation. That's the entire extent of your involvement.
The goal is zero manual order processing on your end — ever again.

Part Five

The Pricing Conversation

This section is here because it matters — even if it's the hardest one. No pressure. Just information worth looking at together, when you're ready.

🌕 What the Data Suggests

You've been selling your work at roughly the same price range for many years. Your pieces consistently sell in under 10 minutes.

In almost any market, that's a signal that the price ceiling hasn't been found yet — that buyers would have paid more. When demand is immediate and universal, it almost always means there's room to grow.

The one person out of thirty who says "nevermind" isn't your collector. Your collectors never say nevermind. And they'd follow you through a price increase without blinking.

🔎 What We'd Like to Do

We're not suggesting a dramatic overnight change. Just:

  • Pull your Etsy sales history and analyze it — which pieces sell fastest, at what prices, in which themes
  • Research comparable hand painted original art on Etsy to see where the market actually sits
  • Build you a simple, clear picture of your own data

Then you decide what to do with it. No pressure. Just information.

🖼️ Print Buyers vs. Original Buyers

One concern worth addressing directly: offering more prints doesn't threaten original sales.

Print buyers and original buyers are almost never the same person. A print buyer wants to own a piece of the art. An original buyer wants to own the art and know nobody else has it. Offering prints actually reinforces that — it signals the original is the real prize.

Disney sells millions of prints of beloved images. It doesn't stop collectors from paying thousands for original animation cels.
